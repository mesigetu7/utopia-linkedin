A building I drove past recently has a beautiful entrance and a staircase that fights it the entire way.

Grand lobby. Calm, generous proportions. Then a staircase bolted into the middle of it like it was chosen from a different project entirely. Wrong weight. Wrong language. Technically fine. Completely wrong for the room.

It's never the craftsmanship that fails in moments like this. It's the decision underneath it.

A staircase in an entrance isn't just circulation. It's the first sentence the building says about itself. Get it wrong and everything after it is an apology.

The frustrating part: the right form was almost certainly possible. Sculptural, light, matched to the space. The kind of thing UHPC and GFRC are made for. It just wasn't chosen, because "that shape is hard to build" ends most of these conversations before they start.

If that were your lobby — would you live with it, or tear it out and start the first sentence again?
