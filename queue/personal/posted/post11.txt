"Can you actually make that?"

It's the question I get most. Usually said while pointing at something curved, twisted, perforated, or shaped like nothing concrete is "supposed" to look like.

The honest answer is almost always yes. And the reason is simple: with GFRC and UHPC, the difficulty isn't in the shape. It's in the mold. Once the mold exists, a wildly complex form casts about as easily as a flat one.

That single fact quietly rewrites the rules for designers here.

Complexity stops being expensive by default. Curves stop being a luxury. The strange, specific, beautiful idea stops being the first thing cut from the budget.

Most design limitations we accept aren't laws of nature. They're just the limits of whatever material we assumed we had to use.

Show me the shape you were told couldn't be done here. I'm genuinely curious what people have been holding back.
