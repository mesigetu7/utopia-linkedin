Ten years from now, someone will drive through Addis and take a building for granted that wasn't possible today.

A facade that moves. A public space that's aged well instead of badly. Forms that didn't have to be flattened to get built. They won't think about the material. People never do, when it works. It'll just look like the way things are.

That's the goal. Not for advanced materials to be impressive. For them to be invisible — the quiet default behind better buildings.

Right now we're in the strange early part, where this still has to be explained, defended, proven project by project. That part doesn't last forever. It never does, with anything that's actually better.

We're not trying to build the most talked-about thing in Ethiopian construction. We're trying to build the thing that's so normal in ten years nobody remembers it was ever a question.

What's something in your field that was "new and risky" once and is now just how it's done?
