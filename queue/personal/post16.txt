Walk past any major construction site in Addis right now and count the cranes.

There are more buildings going up in this city simultaneously than at any point in its history. Hotels, offices, mixed-use towers, residential compounds. The scale is genuinely hard to take in if you step back and look at the skyline compared to five years ago.

What strikes me is this: the decisions being made right now — the materials being specified, the facades being designed, the finishes being chosen — those decisions will define what this city looks like for the next 50 years.

Not the next five. The next fifty.

Ethiopia is not building for the moment. It's building its permanent face to the world.

And most of those decisions are still being made with the same materials and methods that were the default 30 years ago. Not because they're the best option — but because they're the known option.

That gap between what's possible and what's being specified is exactly where we work.

When you look at what's being built in Addis right now — what do you think the city will look like in 2050?
