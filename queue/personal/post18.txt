There's a specific moment in every luxury project when the vision starts to shrink.

The render is ambitious. The design is sharp. The client is excited.

Then the contractor gets involved and starts asking about lead times, site delivery, local availability.

And slowly, quietly, the design gets practical. The curved element becomes flat. The textured facade becomes smooth. The custom stair profile becomes standard. By the time the building is finished, the render and the reality are cousins at best.

This happens on almost every high-end project I observe in Ethiopia. Not because the architects lack ambition. Not because the clients don't want it. But because the materials to execute the vision didn't exist here.

That's the gap we're working to close. When the material can be made locally, delivered on Ethiopian timelines, and cast to match what's in the render — the excuse for simplifying disappears.

The render can just be the building.

Architects — what's the one element you've had to simplify on a project that still bothers you? I'm genuinely curious.
