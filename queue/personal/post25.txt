[NAME: TEST · Demold moment — UHPC]

There's a moment right before you demold a piece when you don't actually know what you have yet.

The mould comes off. And either the surface is exactly what the architect drew — or it isn't.

Watched it happen again this week on a UHPC piece headed for a project here in Addis. Sharp edges. A finish you simply cannot get from ordinary concrete poured on site. No one else in Ethiopia is casting to this tolerance right now — we're still the only ones doing it.

What still surprises me is how many people hear "concrete" and picture something rough, grey, heavy. This is the opposite. Precise. Deliberate. Almost quiet.

The material stopped being the hard part a while ago. Getting the industry to imagine what it can become — that's the real work.

What would you build if the concrete could hold any shape you drew?
