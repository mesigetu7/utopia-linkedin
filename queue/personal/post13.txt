Durability is the most underrated word in Ethiopian construction.

Everyone talks about cost upfront. Almost no one talks about cost over time. So we keep choosing materials that look fine on handover day and start losing the fight with sun, rain, and dust by the second year.

Then we pay again. And again. And we file it under "maintenance" instead of calling it what it is — a design decision that didn't account for where the building actually stands.

GFRC and UHPC aren't interesting because they survive a lab. They're interesting because they survive here. The actual conditions. The real weather. The full lifespan, not the warranty period.

The cheapest material is almost never the one with the lowest price. It's the one you only buy once.

When you evaluate a material, are you pricing the purchase — or the next twenty years?
