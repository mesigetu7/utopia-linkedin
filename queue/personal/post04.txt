There's a third type, and they will never be early.

They won't try a new material because it's better. They'll try it when not using it starts to make them look behind.

For a while this frustrated me. Now I think it's just honest. Most of the market moves this way. They are not paid to be pioneers. They are paid not to be wrong.

So the real question isn't how to convince them. It's what happens to them when UHPC and GFRC stop being "the new thing" in Ethiopia and start being the standard.

Because that shift is already happening. Quietly. Project by project.

The people moving now aren't doing it to be first. They're doing it so they're not the last one explaining why their building looks dated five years early.

When a material becomes the standard, the cost isn't adopting it. It's having waited.
