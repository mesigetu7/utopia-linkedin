Look at the public benches in most Ethiopian corridor projects after one rainy season.

Faded. Cracked. Wobbling. Some already replaced.

We keep installing seating built to lose a fight with our own weather. Then we budget to replace it. Then we act surprised.

GFRC benches don't play that game. They don't fade in the sun or weaken after heavy rain. They're built to sit in a public space for decades, not seasons. And because they're cast in molds, every corridor can have its own identity instead of the same tired catalogue shape.

Public infrastructure shouldn't be designed around how soon it fails. It should be designed around how long everyone forgets it's even there — because it just works.
