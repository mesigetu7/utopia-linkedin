There's a version of this market where UHPC and GFRC become the default in Ethiopia — not the premium option, not the "interesting material that one company makes," just the standard answer for a specific set of applications.

That shift happens the same way every material transition happens. A few early projects. Visible results. Engineers who've specified it once become comfortable specifying it again. Developers who've seen it on a competitor's building want it on theirs.

We're in the early phase of that curve right now. The projects completing this year and next are the ones that will make that shift happen faster or slower.

What I find interesting is that the Followers — the clients who wait for the market to move before they move — are not passive. They're watching. They're tracking which projects used what. They're noting which buildings still look good and which ones don't.

When the shift happens, it won't feel like a shift to them. It will feel like the obvious choice finally became available.

The obvious choice is already available. It just takes a few more completed projects for it to feel that way.
