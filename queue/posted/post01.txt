Walk into almost any new building in Addis right now and look closely at the facade.

Most of them tell the same story. Straight lines. Flat surfaces. The shape the material allowed — not the shape the architect actually wanted.

For years that was the only option here. If you wanted a curved facade, a perforated screen, a form that moved — you imported it. Months of waiting. Costs that killed the design before it left the page.

So architects stopped drawing those forms. Not because they couldn't imagine them. Because they already knew the answer.

That is the part most people miss. The limitation didn't just change what we build. It quietly changed what we allow ourselves to design.

We make UHPC and GFRC here now. In Ethiopia. The forms that used to be a customs problem are now just a production question.

Architects — how many designs have you quietly killed because the material wasn't available here?
