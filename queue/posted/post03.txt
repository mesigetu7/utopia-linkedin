The most common reaction I get is not excitement. It's a pause.

"Show me where this has been done."

For a long time I thought this was hesitation. It isn't. It's discipline. This client has been burned by promises before. They've seen materials fail in Ethiopian conditions — the sun, the rain, the dust, the load. They are not going to risk a project on a brochure.

I've stopped trying to talk this person out of caution. Caution is the right instinct here.

What changes their mind is never an argument. It's a finished project they can stand next to and touch.

Which is why every single thing we complete matters more than anything we say. The work is the proof. There is no shortcut around that, and there shouldn't be.
