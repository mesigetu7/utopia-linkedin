A developer showed me a render last month. Beautiful building. Sweeping curved balconies, a facade that moved like fabric.

Then he said the line I hear constantly: "But we'll have to simplify it for construction."

Simplify. That word costs Ethiopia more good architecture than budget ever has.

The curve gets flattened. The texture gets dropped. The detail that made the render special quietly disappears between design and groundbreaking. And everyone shrugs, because that's just how it's always been here.

It's not a design problem. It's a materials problem wearing a design problem's clothes.

UHPC and GFRC don't care that a shape is complex. A curve and a flat panel are the same difficulty to cast. The mold does the hard part.

The render doesn't have to be the best version of the building anymore. It can just be the building.

Architects — what's one detail you've been forced to "simplify" that still bothers you?
